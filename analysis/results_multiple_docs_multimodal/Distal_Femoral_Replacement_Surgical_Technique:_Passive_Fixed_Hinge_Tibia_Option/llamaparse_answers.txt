ANSWER:
The choices for the tibial component in the setting of a distal femur fracture are:

1. **Rotating hinge – Passive tibia**
2. **Passive fixed hinge tibia**
3. **Rotating hinge – Polyethylene tibia**
4. **Rotating hinge – Metal cased tibia**
5. **Fixed hinge tibia**

### Decision Criteria:
- **Rotating hinge polyethylene tibia**: Used for routine cases.
- **Rotating hinge metal cased tibia**: Suitable for extra-articular resection or difficult revisions.
- **Fixed hinge tibia**: Best for knees with marked instability.

QUESTION:
Based on the document titled Distal Femoral Replacement Surgical Technique: Passive Fixed Hinge Tibia Option from Onkos, answer this question:

When should a distal femoral replacement be used or considered?

ANSWER:
A distal femoral replacement should be considered for:

- Limb sparing and skeletal restorative procedures involving radical resection and replacement of the distal femur.
- Severe arthropathy of the distal femur unresponsive to other treatments.
- Surgical intervention for severe trauma, revision arthroplasties, failed prostheses, and malignant diseases (e.g., osteogenic sarcoma).
- Conditions such as primary bone tumors, secondary tumors in bone, non-neoplastic conditions affecting long bone shafts, failed joint replacements, and failed massive replacements.
